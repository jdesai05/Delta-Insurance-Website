Protection

  • Death benefit: The core feature, providing a financial cushion for your loved ones if you pass away during the policy term.
  • Term life: Pure protection, affordable, covers a specific term (e.g., 10 years, 20 years).
  • Universal life: Combines protection with a cash value component that grows over time.
  • Whole life: Guaranteed coverage for your entire life, also builds cash value.
 

Financial Planning

  • Maturity benefit: Pays out a lump sum when the policy reaches its maturity date (for whole and endowment policies).
  • Cash value accumulation: Some policies accrue cash value that can be accessed through loans or withdrawals (universal and whole life).
  • Riders: Add-ons providing extra benefits like disability income, accidental death, or critical illness coverage.
 

Flexibility and Accessibility

  • Premium options: Choose premium payment terms like monthly, yearly, or single pay.
  • Policy adjustments: Some policies allow adjustments like changing the death benefit or extending the coverage term.
  • Beneficiary designations: Choose who receives the death benefit, with options to change or add beneficiaries over time.
